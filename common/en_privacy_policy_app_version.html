SALTED, LTD (hereinafter referred to as the “company”) establishes and publishes the privacy policy as follows to protect privacy of the users and to deal problems quickly and smoothly according to the Personal Information Protection Act.

Article 1 (Purpose of the Collection and Use of Personal Information items and Sensitive Information)

The company collects and processes personal information and sensitive information (hereinafter referred to as “personal information”) for the following purposes. Processing information is not used for any other purposes than the following and in case the purpose of use is changed, necessary measures will be implemented such as asking for a separate consent.

  • [Membership and Management]Confirmation of membership registration, identification, and authentication according to the provision of the membership service, membership maintenance and management, identification according to the implementation of the limited identification system, prevention of illegal use of services, and consent of a legal representative when processing personal information of children under the age of 14.
  • [Provision of Goods or Services]Service improvement through age, gender, access frequency and statistical analysis etc., provision of personalized contents and optimized service based on estimation of user propensity, new service and contents or product development, analysis of service visit and usage history.
  • [Handling Problems]Identification of the complainant, confirmation of complaints, contact/notice for fact investigation, purpose of notification of handling results.
  • [Notice and Marketing related to Service]Various notices related to the service, marketing and promotion such as provision of event information and advertising information, purpose of determining access frequency.

      • A. Retention according to ˹Act on Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce, etc.˼

  • Paragraph 1. The company processes personal information only within the scope specified in Article 1 (Purpose of the Collection and Use of Personal information items and sensitive information), and is subject to the consent of the information subject and provide personal information to the third party only for legal reasons such as Articles 17 and 18 of the ˹Personal Information Protection Act˼
  • Paragraph 2. The company may provide personal information of the user for below reasons.
    • A. When having received a request from the relevant agency for the purpose of the investigation according to the relevant laws.
    • B. Providing to advertiser partners or research organizations in a form that specific individuals cannot be identified for the purpose of statistical research or market research.
    • C. When having received a request in accordance with the procedures stipulated in other relevant laws.
  • Paragraph 3. In principle, even if personal information is provided in accordance with the relevant laws or at the request of an investigation agency, this shall be notified to the user. There may be cases where it cannot be notified due to unavoidable circumstances according to the related laws, we will do our best not to provide information indiscriminately against the original purpose of collection and use.
  • Paragraph 4. The company may provide personal information of the user for the purpose of technology development or better service provision. In this case, what organization or company to provide personal information to, what information is needed and its reasons, and for how long it will be protected and managed by them shall be notified to the users and ask for consent in advance. When the user disagrees to consent, additional information will not be arbitrarily collected or provided.

Article 5. (Rights and Duties of Users and Legal Representatives and the Methods of Exercise)

  • Paragraph 1. Members can exercise the right to view, correct, delete and suspend processing of personal information to the company at any time.
  • Paragraph 2. In accordance with Article 14, Paragraph 1 of the enforcement decree of the Personal Information Protection Act, the exercise of the rights of Paragraph 1 may be done in writing, e-mail, fax, etc.
  • Paragraph 3. The exercise of the rights under Paragraph 1 can be done through a legal representative of the information subject or a representative that has been delegated. In this case, you must submit a power of attorney in the form of Attachment No. 11 of the Enforcement Regulations of the Personal Information Protection Act.
  • Paragraph 4. In case the client has requested for a deletion of personal information, the processing and retention period of personal information in Article 3 and destruction procedure and method of destruction of personal information in Article 7 shall be followed.

Article 7. (Protection Measures of Personal Information)

The company is taking the following measures to ensure the safety of personal information.

  • Paragraph 1. Administrative measures: Establishment and implementation of an internal management plan including matters related to the organization and operation of a personal information protection organization, such as the designation of a personal information protection officer, and education of personal information handler.
  • Paragraph 2. Technical measures: Granting access to the database system that is systematically configured to handle personal information, establishment and implementation of standards for change and cancellation, installation and operation of intrusion prevention and intrusion detection systems for personal information processing systems, establishment and operation of standards such as the password generation method and change cycle, date and time of access when the personal information handler accesses the personal information processing system and processes personal information, storage, confirmation of objection, and supervision of processing history, backup and storage of access records to the personal information processing system in a separate storage device, one-way encryption of passwords, measures such as establishment of a security server for sending and receiving users’ personal information and authentication information through the information and communications network, installation of vaccine software and regular update and inspection for personal information processing system and personal information handlers to always check and treat malicious programs such as computer viruses and spyware that have penetrated into the information devices used for personal information processing.

Article 8 (Internet Access Information File (Cookie) Settings)

  • Paragraph 1. Definition of Cookies: In order to provide personalized and customized service to individual members in some separate services, the company uses “cookies” that store and retrieve user information from time to time. Cookies are very small data package that the server uses to operate the website sent to the browser, and they are stored on the hard disk of members’ terminal (PC, smartphone, tablet, PC, etc.), and it expires once the browser is closed or members log out.
  • Paragraph 2. Purpose of the Usage of Cookies: Cookies store members’ preferred settings to support a faster web environment for members and use them to improve services for convenient use. With this, members are able to use service more conveniently.
  • Paragraph 3. Installation of Cookies/Operation and Rejection: Members have the choice of cookie installation and they can reject the storage of cookies or delete them any time. When cookie storage is rejected, part of the service or the whole service may not function properly.
  • Paragraph 4. Cookie Settings Reject Method: Members can decide whether to allow or reject information collection by cookies through our security policy. Members can set their browser to reject cookies through Help on the browser toolbar.
  • Paragraph 5. For more information on how to manage Flash cookies, please refer to the ‘Cookies’ section at the bottom of the initial screen of Adobe website. Members can manage cookies through the settings function on their mobile devices.
